An overview of the issue:

New York City is notorious for its lack of available parking spaces. A city that is known for its liveliness and fast-paced lifestyle, the city's parking spaces do not seem to be keeping up with its residents needs. While some condominiums provide reserved parking spaces at the price of an annual fee (around $165), most residents, who are also car owners and can not afford such high fees, are forced to park their vehicles on the streets. With this problem on the rise, it has become harder to manage a vehicle, especially for those belonging to low-income households.

Analysis

Let us assume, for the sake of simplicity, that the rent prices have increased by $50 each year (as is standard in most rented condominium apartments) since 2019. Therefore,
these are the current monthly rent for the following apartments in New York City

Studio Apartment is about $2560
One Bedroom Apartment is about $3160
And a Two Bedroom Apartment is about $3840

According to the "United States Census Bureau" the annual median income of the average New Yorker is $32,320. Assuming this individual rents a studio apartment, every year
they pay $30, 720 on rent. Thus, every year they are left with $(32,320 - 30,720) = $1600.
Disregarding other expenses (bills, personal expenses), this individual can only afford to pay at most 26 (assuming they are $60 each) or 12 (assuming they are $125 eacg) parking tickets in a year.
If this individual opts for paid parking lot slots instead, then each year they have $(1600 - 165) = $1435 to themselves.
This is a serious problem, and the scenario (where we temporarily ignored bills, and other medical or personal expenses) is unrealistic. However, this is the reality for many low-income New York residents. For many of them, having to pay 3 consecutive parking tickets may cause them to fail to pay their rent.
